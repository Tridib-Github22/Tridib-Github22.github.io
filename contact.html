Frequently Asked Questions

Does acupuncture hurt?

Most patients feel little to no pain during acupuncture. The needles used are hair-thin — much finer than hypodermic needles. You may feel a slight tingling, warmth, or dull ache at the needle site, which is a positive sign of the treatment working. Most patients find it deeply relaxing.

How many sessions will I need?

The number of sessions varies by condition and individual response. Acute conditions may resolve in 3–6 sessions, while chronic conditions typically require 8–12 or more. Dr. He will provide a personalized treatment plan after your initial consultation and adjust it based on your progress.

Do you accept insurance?

We accept many insurance plans that include acupuncture coverage. Please call us at 919-491-8983 or email info@pingtcm.com to verify your specific coverage before your first appointment. We also offer flexible self-pay rates and treatment packages.

What should I expect on my first visit?

Your first visit includes a comprehensive health intake (45–60 minutes) where Dr. He reviews your medical history, current symptoms, and lifestyle factors. He may examine your tongue and pulse — key diagnostic tools in TCM — before creating your personalized treatment plan. The first acupuncture treatment often follows the consultation.

Is TCM safe to combine with conventional medicine?

Yes, in most cases TCM works very well alongside conventional Western medicine. Dr. He will review your current medications and treatments to ensure there are no contraindications. Many patients use TCM to complement their ongoing medical care, often reducing their need for pharmaceuticals over time.
